## Overall Impression

This is a well-crafted, locally-fluent charity marathon page that already survived a heavy performance/accessibility/polish pass — the craft shows in the details (working date-aware roadmap, real USSD payment conventions, genuinely considered typography). The score is dragged down almost entirely by one thing: the Register section confidently instructs users through a payment flow that isn't live yet, and buries the one thing that *does* work (WhatsApp registration) beneath two large, non-functional payment cards. Fix that one section's sequencing and hierarchy, and this jumps from "Acceptable" to "Good."

## What's Working

1. **The roadmap's date-aware logic actually runs correctly in the browser** — Arua (19 Sep 2026) is live-marked "NEXT UP" with a pulsing ring against today's date, past items are dimmed with checkmarks. Real functional/design integration, not decoration.
2. **Hero composition** — diagonal Uganda-flag clip-path, faint AIDS-ribbon watermark, noise texture, and photo gradient layer well without feeling busy; the red "MARATHON" color-break mid-heading is a nice hook (contrast issue aside, see Priority Issues).
3. **Local payment fluency** — real USSD dial codes, and Airtel/MTN cards correctly use each brand's own color rather than forcing the site's red-first palette onto them.

## Priority Issues

**[P0] Payment section instructs a flow that doesn't work.** The four numbered steps ("Dial *185#... Enter Merchant ID... confirm") sit directly beneath two cards reading "Merchant ID: Announced Soon." A first-time user could start dialing before realizing the flow is incomplete, and a skeptical user (Riley) who catches the dead end will start distrusting the "500 kit slots available" urgency claim right above it too — the fake-flow feeling bleeds into other honest claims on the same panel.
*Why it matters:* This is the highest-stakes moment on the page (the conversion point) actively working against itself.
*Fix:* Either visually gray out/disable the numbered steps until merchant IDs are live, or lead the section with a single unmissable "Mobile Money launching soon — register via WhatsApp today" banner above the payment cards, not after them.
*Suggested command:* `/impeccable clarify`

**[P1] Hero headline fails contrast in the "MARATHON" word.** Detector-confirmed: `#cc0000` text on an `#8b0000`-tinted region of the hero gradient measures ~1.7:1, well under the 3:1 minimum WCAG allows even for large display text.
*Why it matters:* This is the single largest, most prominent text on the entire page — the exact place contrast should be bulletproof.
*Fix:* Either lighten the `em` color for that word specifically, add a subtle text-shadow/darker gradient stop behind it, or shift the gradient so the red word never sits over the deep-red zone.
*Suggested command:* `/impeccable polish`

**[P1] Race Info section is 75% placeholder.** Categories, Venue, and Kit Collection all read "TO BE ANNOUNCED" directly under the confident eyebrow "Know Before You Go" — right after the strongest section on the page (About), this is the first real emotional dip.
*Why it matters:* A page proudly running a 140-day countdown clock, followed immediately by "we don't know the venue yet," reads as under-prepared rather than "details coming."
*Fix:* Either hold this section until at least 2 of 4 fields are confirmed, or soften the eyebrow copy to set the right expectation ("Details Confirming Soon" instead of "Know Before You Go").
*Suggested command:* `/impeccable clarify`

**[P1] The one CTA that works is visually the smallest.** In the register section, the working WhatsApp registration button is a small green button with a gray caption, positioned *after* two large, brightly-colored payment cards that currently do nothing.
*Why it matters:* The single action a user can actually complete right now has the least visual weight in the section.
*Fix:* Swap the visual hierarchy — give WhatsApp the primary-CTA treatment until mobile money is actually live, and demote the payment cards to a smaller "coming soon" strip.
*Suggested command:* `/impeccable layout`

**[P2] Slider pause button overlaps the caption text.** Confirmed in the live desktop render: `.sl-pause` (bottom:14px, left:18px) sits directly on top of the "R" in "RUN FOR AWARENESS."
*Why it matters:* Minor but sloppy — a control visually colliding with content undermines the otherwise-careful polish elsewhere.
*Fix:* Move `.sl-pause` to stack with the dots on the bottom-right, or increase the caption's left padding to clear it.
*Suggested command:* `/impeccable layout`

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (First-Timer), learning about the race then trying to register:** Scrolls past a strong hero and About section into Race Info expecting logistics, and hits "TO BE ANNOUNCED" three times under "Know Before You Go" — actively erodes confidence built two sections earlier. Later opens the FAQ and finds the honest payment answer buried there, when it should be surfaced louder in the Register section itself.

**Riley (Stress-Tester), trying to actually pay:** Reads the 4 numbered payment steps as real instructions, gets to step 3 ("Enter Merchant ID"), finds no ID on either card. Now questions whether "500 kit slots available" is real either — a fake-feeling payment flow contaminates trust in the honest scarcity messaging sitting right above it.

**Casey (Distracted Mobile User):** At 375px, thumb naturally lands where WhatsApp would be, but the eye is pulled first to the two large, high-contrast, non-functional payment cards (bigger, positioned first). Real risk of bouncing before scrolling down to the one CTA that actually works.
